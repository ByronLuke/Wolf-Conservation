About Us

Our mission is to ensure the long-term survival of Gray Wolves in California through a comprehensive approach that includes scientific research, public education, and advocacy. We believe that by understanding and addressing the challenges facing Gray Wolves, we can promote coexistence and conservation. Our team is dedicated to the successful reintegration of Gray Wolves into their historical habitats in the Sierra Mountains. This effort involves collaboration with national parks, wildlife experts, and government agencies to create a suitable and sustainable environment for the wolves.

We prioritize the use of nonlethal deterrents and noninvasive monitoring technologies to minimize human-wolf conflicts and to gather important data on wolf behavior and population dynamics. These methods help us to protect both the wolves and the interests of local communities. Through public education and advocacy, we aim to raise awareness about the importance of Gray Wolves in the ecosystem and to foster a greater understanding of their role in maintaining biodiversity. Join us in our efforts to protect and conserve these magnificent creatures for future generations.

About the Founder

Byron Praetor-Cunningham, our founder, is an Environmental Scientist and Applications Developer with a passion for conservation. Born and raised in South Africa, Byron's journey has been shaped by a deep connection to nature and a commitment to environmental stewardship. A veteran of the Marine Corps, Byron developed a profound love for wolves while stationed in California. Leveraging his unique background in environmental science and technology, Byron started this organization to protect and promote the well-being of Gray Wolves. His vision is to integrate modern technology with traditional conservation methods to create a harmonious coexistence between humans and wolves. Byron's dedication to this cause is driven by his personal experiences and a desire to make a lasting impact on wildlife conservation.
