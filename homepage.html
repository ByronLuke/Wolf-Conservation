Wolf Conservation Management Plan for The California Gray Wolf

Gray Wolves: Conservation and Coexistence

Ensuring the long-term survival of Gray Wolves in California through scientific research, public education, and advocacy.

Goals & Objectives of the Conservation Plan

Successfully integrating Gray Wolves in Sierra Mountains

Historical evidence shows that this was also the last known home to wolves in California, after they were previously inhabiting San Diego coastal region. Their move from coastal regions in San Diego was due to humans colonizing and exploring and with their only predator being present they began to either be killed or disperse for safety. The San Diego region being so industrialized there is no space, so the Sierra Mountains is the second-best option in trying to maintain some type of adherence to historical habitats and best suitable environment.
This can be achieved by coordinating with Yosemite National Park wolf authorities and experts in ensuring a smooth transition of wolves, identifying best area for the wolves to flourish, and gaining funding from The California Fish and Wildlife Services to provide sufficient capital to make this a reality

The integration of nonlethal deterrent methods for wolves currently in California

There needs to be nonlethal deterrents put in place and used by farmers in California currently whilst the move is still in the preparation stage. There are small number of wolves currently in that region and there have already been killings of wolves in California and they haven’t been present long, one being attributed to a rancher in Northern California after it was spotted feeding on one of the livestock.

The integration, sole-use of, or further explored use of noninvasive Wolf Howl monitoring technology

The goal is to implement the use of non-invasive wolf howl monitoring technology as an effective replacement for the usual ‘collar-method’ whereby wolves get captured, sedated, and fitted with collar tracking technology. The technology behind acoustic monitoring will be further elaborated on later in the plan along with supporting evidence.
The implementation of microphones in the areas prior to wolf reintegration in the selected area gives us time to prepare for these wolves. This not only provides us the opportunity to utilize a noninvasive alternative to the collar method. Additionally, the wolves that are moved to that region will be inevitably captured to make this move possible, during capture sedation is inevitable, meaning we can use this inevitable period to place collars on the new wolves in that region. We will then be able to utilize the new technology in collaboration with the collar-method to prevent future need for capturing as the acoustic monitoring technologies will already be in place.

Technology that will be utilised
  • Testo816 sound lever meter
  • Songmeter SM2+
  • Victory PRF Laser Rangefinder
