Summary of Key Components and Actions in the California Gray Wolf Wildlife Management Plan
Category Details Actions Strategies
Wolf Age Stages Cubs rely on milk and regurgitated food; adolescents begin hunting; adults maintain diet and habitat. Ensure safe habitats for cubs, monitor adolescent hunting behavior, support adults with pack integration.
Threats Current threats include capturing by humans and conflicts with farmers; future threats involve diseases like rabies and canine parvovirus, and continued human intervention. Educate farmers on non-lethal deterrents, implement non-invasive monitoring techniques, ensure vaccination and health checks for wolves.
Management Strategies Focus on non-invasive acoustic monitoring, relocation to safer habitats like the Sierra Mountains, and using non-lethal deterrents to protect livestock. Coordinate with Yosemite for relocation, set up acoustic monitoring stations, train farmers in non-lethal methods, and seek funding for ongoing conservation efforts.
